# VibeBoard Launch Strategy

Compiled from Riley Walz (@rtwlz), Pieter Levels (@levelsio), Garry Tan (@garrytan), Andrej Karpathy (@karpathy), and the broader indie-maker / Show HN crowd (Nov 2025–Apr 2026).

Each insight is drawn from a specific tweet, talk, or launch documented in public. "Apply to VibeBoard" turns each lesson into a concrete action for launch day.

---

## 1. Launch one weird, over-specific thing instead of "a platform"

Riley Walz's hits are never "platforms" — they are laser-specific, often uncomfortably so: a Gmail for Epstein's emails, a Find-My for parking cops, a site that ranks restaurants by attractive patrons. Each one reads as a single sentence that makes you immediately want to click.

**Example:** Jmail — "we cloned Gmail, except you're logged in as Epstein and can see his emails." One sentence, one link, went viral across Fast Company, PC Gamer, Gizmodo in 48 hours. [SF Standard coverage](https://sfstandard.com/2025/11/21/epstein-emails-san-francisco-jmail/)

**Apply to VibeBoard:** Do not launch as "a showcase for AI-built tools." Launch as "the front page of things a human built in a weekend with Claude last month." One screenshot, one tagline, one URL.

---

## 2. The headline IS the product

Levels, Walz, and Karpathy all ship with a single-sentence hook tuned for retweets. Karpathy's MenuGen launch tweet is literally "I attended a vibe coding hackathon and built a web app (auth, payments, deploy)" — a brag dressed as a hackathon story. The hook, not the landing page, is what travels.

**Example:** Karpathy's MenuGen announcement: [x.com/karpathy/status/1917961248031080455](https://x.com/karpathy/status/1917961248031080455)

**Apply to VibeBoard:** Write the launch tweet BEFORE you finish building. If you can't compress VibeBoard to a one-line hook a stranger would retweet, you're not ready to ship. Draft candidates: "Built by humans. Coded by Claude. Voted by you."

---

## 3. Build in public with real numbers, not vibes

Pieter Levels posts his literal Stripe revenue screenshots. This both drives accountability and makes every milestone a new tweet. Followers feel invested and amplify each update.

**Example:** Levels' public revenue dashboard and recurring "$XK MRR" posts — regular pattern across [@levelsio](https://x.com/levelsio).

**Apply to VibeBoard:** From day one publish the submission count, upvote count, top tool, and new-submission rate. A weekly "VibeBoard this week" post with numbers = free recurring content.

---

## 4. Ship fast, ship flawed, let the audience QA it

Levels' "12 startups in 12 months" and Walz's 5-hour Jmail build both demonstrate: the bar is "does the thing work for the first person who clicks it" not "is it polished." Shipping flawed and iterating in public is itself the marketing.

**Example:** Walz + Igel built the first version of Jmail in about 5 hours ([Fast Company feature](https://www.fastcompany.com/91495585/jmail-engineers-epstein-emails)).

**Apply to VibeBoard:** Go live with the ugliest version that lets people submit and upvote. Tweet every bug fix and new feature for 14 days. Treat launch week as a dev stream.

---

## 5. Use ultra-short demo clips — 10-second screencaps beat explainer videos

Posts with visuals get roughly 2x the engagement of text-only posts, and short auto-playing clips outperform long demos. Levels' Fly launch clip was a ~15-second loop of a plane flying with multiplayer ghosts — nothing more.

**Example:** Levels' Fly reveal threads on X (embedded loops, no narration) — see [Fly showcase](https://www.vibecoding.wiki/showcase/fly-pieter-com-by-levelsio/).

**Apply to VibeBoard:** Launch tweet = one 10-15s screen recording showing a card flip, an upvote, and a new submission animating in. No voiceover. No cuts. Just motion.

---

## 6. Seed the feed before you announce — never launch empty

Walz's sites always launch with a full gallery of content already in them (thousands of emails, full playlists, pre-scanned graffiti). There is no "empty state." Empty platforms on X die instantly.

**Example:** Jmail launched with every released Epstein email already indexed and searchable on day one.

**Apply to VibeBoard:** Do NOT launch until you have 30+ real seeded submissions (the other file in this folder). First-time visitors must see an already-alive feed.

---

## 7. Make it a leaderboard or game — people love being ranked

The Vibe Jam worked because it had a Gold/Silver/Bronze and $40K in prizes. That framing turned a showcase into a sport. Creators promoted their own entries to climb the rankings, which promoted the jam itself for free.

**Example:** Levels' 2025 Vibe Coding Game Jam drew 1000+ submissions — announcement thread: [x.com/levelsio/status/1901660771505021314](https://x.com/levelsio/status/1901660771505021314).

**Apply to VibeBoard:** Lean into "Top 10 this week" and "Maker of the Week" leaderboards. Publish the weekly winners as their own tweet with a tagged creator — they will retweet.

---

## 8. Garry Tan's "show don't tell": post the product, not the pitch

Garry Tan has been loud that in the AI era, founders win on UX quality, and he "can tell immediately" from a demo. His retweets almost always quote a founder who posted a live URL + screenshot, not a pitch deck.

**Example:** [x.com/garrytan/status/1786926287954805201](https://x.com/garrytan/status/1786926287954805201) — "Founders must learn how to make good user experiences again."

**Apply to VibeBoard:** Every time you tweet VibeBoard, include: live URL + screenshot of a real card. Never a roadmap. Never a "coming soon." Tag Garry Tan on the one tweet where the UI is strongest; he retweets well-crafted indie projects.

---

## 9. Tap existing tribes — don't try to invent an audience

Levels didn't invent a "digital nomads" audience; he found them on Hacker News and crystallized them. Walz's sites spread through San Francisco Twitter circles first, then jumped. Karpathy's MenuGen reached 100K views because the "vibe coding" tribe was already primed for it.

**Example:** Karpathy's knowledge-bases post went viral through the pre-existing vibe-coding tribe — [x.com/karpathy/status/2040470801506541998](https://x.com/karpathy/status/2040470801506541998).

**Apply to VibeBoard:** Target three existing tribes in the first week: (1) Cursor/Claude Code users (tag @cursor_ai, @AnthropicAI), (2) indie-hackers subreddit, (3) Levels' replies and quote-retweet-worthy threads. Don't post into the void.

---

## 10. Make the site itself shareable — every page should have a tweet button

Walz's sites are built so any entity inside them (an email, a parking cop, a playlist) has its own unique URL and an OG image. This turns every single item into a potential viral post. VibeBoard should treat every submission card the same way.

**Example:** Jmail emails each have their own shareable URL with a preview card. [Fast Company on Jmail engineers](https://www.fastcompany.com/91495585/jmail-engineers-epstein-emails)

**Apply to VibeBoard:** Every submission gets a clean URL (`vibeboard.app/t/fly-pieter-com`), a proper OG image with screenshot + name + creator, and a one-click "Share on X" button pre-filled with the tool's punchy description. The site should manufacture its own tweets.

---

## Launch day checklist — first 24 hours

1. **T-24h:** Seed the database with 30 verified tools (see `seed-submissions.md`). Confirm every card has a screenshot, tag set, and creator handle.
2. **T-2h:** Pre-write 6 tweets: the launch hook, 3 highlight cards ("today we shipped with X"), one revenue/stats post, one thank-you to makers whose tools were seeded. Schedule the launch hook for a Tuesday/Wednesday 10am Pacific (when indie-hacker X is most active).
3. **T-0 (launch tweet):** One sentence + one 10-15s screencap + the URL. No thread. Pin it.
4. **T+15min:** DM every creator whose tool you seeded with a direct link to their VibeBoard card. Ask them to retweet — they usually will.
5. **T+1h:** Post the "behind the build" reply under your pinned tweet: the stack, the timeline ("built in N days with Claude Code"), one honest screenshot of a bug you hit.
6. **T+4h:** Cross-post to Show HN with a title like "Show HN: VibeBoard — a community showcase for AI-built weekend projects." Keep the first comment ready with context and the URL.
7. **T+12h:** Quote-tweet the best new organic submission with a "Top of VibeBoard today:" screenshot. Repeat daily for two weeks.
8. **T+24h:** Post stats — submissions count, upvote count, top-3 tools, and the creator whose tool won the day. Tag all three creators. This becomes the template for every weekly recap.
